Day 4 built capability — Day 5 raises the stakes

From a forgiving world to a high-stakes one

💬 The forgiving world (so far)

  • A wrong draft is edited; a mis-routed ticket is re-routed
  • Errors are small, cheap & catchable
  • Great for learning the loop and shipping everyday value

⚠️ The high-stakes world (today)

  • Finance: a wrong figure or an unsupervised trade — millions, and regulation
  • Health: a wrong inference harms a patient; data is protected by law
  • Cyber: a missed alert is a breach; a bad auto-fix takes down production

🧠 Same capability, higher consequence. Day 5 isn’t new AI — it’s deploying the agents you have where mistakes are expensive, wrapped in the rigor and oversight that makes them safe.

What becomes non-negotiable

The new bar for a high-stakes agent

🎯 Accuracy & grounding

Every claim correct & traceable to a source — no hallucinated figures.

📜 Auditability

Show why it said what it said — sources, steps, figures.

⚖️ Compliance

Respect the domain’s rules — financial, health-privacy, security.

🔒 Privacy

Sensitive data minimized, protected, never leaked externally.

👤 Human oversight

A qualified human owns any consequential decision.

🧠 Same skills, mandatory

Grounding, structure, validation, HITL — now required, not optional.

💡 You already learned every one of these on Days 3–4. The domain doesn’t need new techniques — it raises the floor and makes them mandatory.

The worked example · your Lab 5.1

The financial-report insight agent, end to end

The run, made visible

The insight agent’s trace

Trigger: Q3 report for ACME Corp — analyze key figures. Thought: I need the actual numbers, not my memory. Action: extract_figure("revenue") Observation: {value: 120.0M, source: "p4, income stmt"} Action: extract_figure("net_income") Observation: {value: 9.0M, source: "p4, income stmt"} Thought: Compute YoY vs last year (107.1M) and the margin. Action: compute("(120-107.1)/107.1*100") -> 12.0% revenue growth Draft insight: "Revenue +12% YoY [p4]; net margin 7.5%, down from 9.1% [p4] -- FLAG." Validation: OK -- every figure cited; no buy/sell language. Final: status="needs_review" # analysis only -- no recommendation, no trade tool

👉 The output is analysis + a “needs review” flag, every figure cited to the document — never a recommendation. An analyst can click straight to the source for any number.

Illustrative · the shape of Lab 5.1

The insight agent, in code

# Tools read & compute. Note what's MISSING: no place_trade, no give_advice. @tool def extract_figure(name: str) -> dict: """Pull a reported figure AND its source location from the filing.""" return report.lookup(name) # {value, source} -- grounded, cited tools = [extract_figure, compute] # read-only -- the agent CANNOT trade or advise agent = create_react_agent(llm, tools, insight_prompt) executor = AgentExecutor(agent=agent, tools=tools, max_iterations=8) insight = executor.invoke({"input": report}) # structured, CITED summary assert insight.every_figure_cited() # auditability guardrail send_to_analyst(insight) # a human reviews & decides

👉 In a high-stakes domain your strongest safety control is the tool you don’t provide. No place_trade, no give_advice — the agent can only read, compute & summarize. A human owns the decision.

Healthcare · assist the clinician

Healthcare — help, and hard lines

📝 Documentation

Summarize notes, draft discharge summaries — ease the paperwork burden.

📚 Literature & guidelines

Retrieve & summarize research / care guidelines — grounded & cited.

🩺 Triage support

Gather symptoms, structure intake, surface what a clinician should see.

📅 Admin

Scheduling, prior-auth drafts, code suggestions — for human confirmation.

⛔ Hard lines: never autonomously diagnose or prescribe; patient data is PHI (protected by law) — minimize & secure it; a licensed clinician is always in the loop. Health agents are decision support, never the decision-maker.

The principle for the whole day

Assistive, not autonomous

⛔ Autonomous (wrong)

  • Reads symptoms → outputs a diagnosis / prescription
  • Acts as if it were the clinician
  • Unsafe, often illegal — and fails on the rare case

✅ Assistive (right)

  • Pulls history, summarizes notes, retrieves the guideline
  • Surfaces a cited briefing — the clinician examines & decides
  • Most of the value, with a qualified human in control

🧠 The pattern across every high-stakes domain: the agent does the 90% of grounded legwork; the human owns the 10% that’s judgement. Assistive-not-autonomous is what makes these agents deployable at all.

Cross-cutting 1 · ground everything

Grounding: RAG & citations

In these domains the model’s memory isn’t just unreliable — it’s dangerous. The agent must answer only from retrieved, authoritative sources.

🔧 Tools for facts

Exact figures from the systems of record — the filing, the record, the log.

📚 RAG for knowledge

Retrieve the relevant passages (guidelines, policy, intel); reason over those.

🔖 Citations

Every claim points to its source — verifiable by a human, traceable by an auditor.

🧠 Day 3’s RAG and Module 7’s gather-first, now mandatory. The rule for high-stakes agents: if it isn’t grounded and cited, it doesn’t ship.

Cross-cutting 2 · prove how it got there

Auditability: structure & the trail

🔢 Structured output

Every conclusion a defined field with its citation — individually inspectable & checkable (Module 7).

📋 The audit trail

Log the whole run — trigger, tool calls, figures, reasoning, output, human decision (Module 7–8).

🧠 Together: a complete, replayable record — for any conclusion, point to the exact source & step. It satisfies compliance, and the trail is how you debug & improve the agent. An unauditable high-stakes agent is a non-starter.

Cross-cutting 3 · the sensitive data

Privacy, compliance & data handling

✂️ Minimize

Send only what the task needs — redact / tokenize identifiers.

🔒 Protect

Encrypt, control access, and be deliberate about where data goes (private / on-prem).

⚖️ Comply

Know the regime — HIPAA, financial & security rules — and design for it.

🗃️ Guard the logs

Audit trails hold sensitive data too — same protection.

💡 It’s why Day 1 started on local models: a public API may be the wrong place for PHI or MNPI. Data handling is a first-class design constraint — decide where the data can go before you build.

The golden rule of Day 5

Assistive on judgement, autonomous on legwork

⚡ Autonomous on the safe 90%

Gather, ground, compute, summarize, draft, recommend — none of it irreversible.

👤 Human on the risky 10%

The trade, the diagnosis, the isolation, the money movement — a qualified human owns it.

👉 Enforce it the simplest way — the Module 7 move: don’t give the agent the dangerous tool. No trade, no prescribe, no auto-remediate. Withholding the capability beats any prompt instruction. Assistive, not autonomous — enforced by what you don’t build.

Choosing the use case well

When to trust an agent here — and when not

✅ A good high-stakes fit

  • Grounded & checkable — traceable to a source
  • Assistive — it informs a human who decides
  • Errors catchable — reviewed, reversible or gated
  • Real value — saves expert time, surfaces what humans miss

⛔ A trap

  • Demands an autonomous, irreversible decision
  • No human in the loop
  • A single wrong output causes irreversible harm
  • Autonomous trading / diagnosis / remediation

🧠 The senior move: decompose. Hand the agent the grounded, assistive, checkable parts; keep the consequential decision with the human. Automate the legwork, elevate the human to the judgement.

Being wrong here is a headline, not a ticket

Failure modes & the cost of being wrong

💩 Hallucinated facts

An invented figure stated confidently — defended by grounding + citation.

🤖 Automation bias

Humans rubber-stamp a confident machine — make it show its work.

📤 Data leakage

Sensitive data sent where it shouldn’t — minimize & control the flow.

📉 Silent drift

Formats / guidelines change, quality slips — caught by monitoring.

🧠 Every discipline today — grounding, citations, structure, audit trails, privacy, and a human on consequential decisions — exists to lower the odds and contain the blast radius of an error. Make mistakes grounded, visible, catchable & non-catastrophic. Module 10 goes deeper.
