softmax(z) — turning scores into weights

Attention scores each word, then softmax turns those raw scores into weights that sum to 1. Drag the scores and the temperature below and watch the two steps happen live — exponentiate, then normalise. The defaults reproduce cat’s attention row from Lab 3.3.

formula e^(zᵢ/T) ÷ Σ e^(z⫺/T) always > 0 Σ weights = 1 in attention T = √d

softmax(zᵢ) = e^(zᵢ/T) ÷ Σj e^(zj/T) — the exact line you wrote in Lab 3.3

① Exponentiate

e^(z/T) makes every score positive and blows up the gaps — a small lead in z becomes a big lead after exp.

② Normalise

Divide by the total so the weights sum to 1. That is what makes them a probability distribution — a weighted average you can trust.

🌡️ Temperature T

Low T → sharpen (winner-take-all); high T → flatten (near-uniform). Attention’s /√d scaling is a temperature.

🔑 This is attention

These weights are exactly attention’s middle step. See it in deck slide 9 and build it in Lab 3.3.
