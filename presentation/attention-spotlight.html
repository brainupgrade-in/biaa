Module 3 · the intuition behind attention

Attention is a spotlight

At a noisy party you don’t hear every voice equally — you lock onto the one that matters and let the rest blur. A model reads a sentence the same way: every word shines a spotlight on the words that explain it.

🎉🗣️

The cocktail-party effect. Ten people are talking at once. Your friend says your name across the room — instantly it cuts through, and the background chatter fades. Your brain just did attention: it weighted one voice up and the others down. Below, each word is a voice in the room — click one to see which words it listens to.

The sentence Click a word — thicker, brighter beams = more attention. Start with “it”.

💡 Why a spotlight?

A word’s meaning depends on context. “It” is empty until you look back at what it points to. Attention is that look.

📏 Distance doesn’t matter

The spotlight jumps directly to the right word — near or far. That is the long-range problem RNNs could not solve.

⚖️ It’s a weighting

The beams are weights that sum to 1 — a little to many, a lot to a few. The exact numbers come from softmax.
