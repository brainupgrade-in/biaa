Why one comma?

(64,) is a one-item tuple — one hidden layer. (64,32) would stack two layers. There is no zero in it; the ,) is just Python punctuation.

Fully connected

Every input pixel links to every hidden neuron, and every neuron to every output. That is where the 4,096 + 640 weights come from — the numbers training tunes.

The hidden layer's job

Its 64 neurons learn reusable strokes — loops, edges, crossbars. The output layer just votes on which digit those strokes add up to.

This is not a mock-up. The weights above were exported from a real MLPClassifier(hidden_layer_sizes=(64,)) trained on scikit-learn's load_digits (the same dataset and settings as Lab 1.11), scoring 97.6% on unseen digits. The forward pass you trigger runs those exact weights in your browser — its prediction matches scikit-learn's on every test sample, mistakes included.
